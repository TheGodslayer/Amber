Introduction:

    Character Creation in this system is simple. I've tried to make this system so that most character concepts are possible. Everyone gets the same points, (Even the NPC's and Elders for the most part. You'll encounter many people with LESS points then you, but even Benedict will be built on the same number of points as you, although Dworkin may not.) Things like Age can drastically raise a characters skill levels. Characters shouldn't have a problem making the character they want unless they just have an unreasonable concept.

  Step 1)Think of a concept. Are you from Amber, Chaos, or Shadow? What do you do? What Skills will you need?

  Step 2) Write a brief background for your character. Nothing fancy. Just give me enough info to know what you want to make and where he or she is from.

  Step 3) Figure out your characters points. For this game you'll have the following points:

Attributes: 10/7/5
Abilities : 21/15/9
Advantages: 15
Backgrounds: 15
Freebies: 25

  All Attributes start out at 4, and go to 10. To set your points, assign each category 10, 7, or 5 points and then split these points up between the 3 attributes in each category
    For example: Let's say we want to create Flora under this system. Obviously most of her attribute points would fall into Social, so we set 10 points into Social, placing 6 in Appearance. (Giving her a 10. All Attributes start out at 4.) 3 in Charisma, (giving her a 7.) And 1 in Manipulation, (giving her a 5.)
    Next Flora places 7 points into her Mental Attributes, placing 2 in Perception and Wits, (giving her a 6 in both.) And 3 points in Intelligence, (giving her a 7.)
    Last Flora places 5 points in her Physical Attributes, placing 3 in Stamina, (giving her a 7.) And 1 in Strength and Dexterity, raising them both to 5.
    A good Start, and Flora can always raise a few of her Attributes with Freebie Points.

  Step 5)
Age

Determine your characters Age. This has to be worked out with the GM and will play a large role in your characters power level and skills. Age works differently for Lords of Amber and Chaos. The rules for age are:

 
Amber Age: 1 Advantage point per Level.

Those of the Blood of Amber have almost limitless potential to learn new skills and improve on them. Their power over Pattern allows them to travel to many different worlds and learn new skills, and face new challenges. It is because of this that they have become among the most skilled and talented beings in Shadow.

Each level of Age equals 150 years for a Lord of Amber. An Amberite with Age adds 1/2 of his Age to all skills. The max level in any skill for an Amberite is 5 + 1/2 of their Age.

The Age attribute can also be used any time a character runs into a situation where he needs a skill that he doesn't have on his character sheet. If it's a situation where the character may have at some time in the past trained in the skill needed then the character can use 1/2 of his Age attribute + whatever Attribute would apply.

Chaos Age: 1 Advantage point per Level.

The Shadows near the Abyss are stagnant with Chaos. In the Courts nothing is constant, the land changes before your eyes and is never the same way twice. Shadow there is easily manipulated, even human level willpower is enough to force a change in Shadow in Chaos. These changes only last while energy is devoted to them however. Because of this environment of constant change, nothing really ever changes. There is little progress, no advances in technology, and little stable resources. The only constant in Chaos is the need to survive.

For the Lords of Chaos time and dates have little meaning, age is often recognized, as is blood and power and these Levels more then any other will determine a Lord of Chaos's standing in the Courts. As the Lords of Chaos age they grow in personal power gaining greater mastery in Shape Shifting and the Logrus. Sorcery is also a common Level in Chaos and the Courts are home to many of the greatest Necromancers and Sorcerers in all of shadow.

Age for Lords of Chaos is not measured in years but instead is measured in Ages, periods of time in Chaos that were subject to great changes such as the creation of the Pattern or Rise of the Houses. It should be noted that on average the Lords of Chaos are MUCH older then the elders in Amber. The difference between say 6 levels of Chaos Age and 7 might well be over a hundred thousand years.

Unlike Amber, skills in Sorcery, the Logrus, Shape Shifting, and other Powers are freely taught there. Lords of Chaos increase in knowledge of these powers as they age. For Each level of Age a Lord of Chaos gains 1 level in a minor power such a Sorcery or Shape Shifting. For every 2 levels or Age a Lord of Chaos also gains 1 level in a Major Power, Which should probably always be Logrus.

  Step 7)
Backgrounds

You get 15 points to spend in backgrounds. Note: Backgrounds are gained and lost by role-playing. There's no guarantee that you'll keep them and there's nothing to stop you from gaining them once the game has started. Once the game has started gaining backgrounds cost no points and losing backgrounds doesn't men you get the points back. It's your characters job to keep what he has and gain what he wants.

Allies: Each Level is an Ally in either Amber or Chaos. Merlin would have Luke as an Ally, probably Nayda and Coral too. Maybe Mandor also.
Military: Each Level increases the amount or quality of troops you may have gathered out in shadow to start.
Shadows: Each Level increases the value, importance and power of the shadow or shadows of your Choice.
Artifacts: Each Level equals some item of power you've been given. 1 Level's a Magic Acorn, 10's something like Grayswandir. Don't get crazy.
Creatures: Each Level is a creature or creatures of power under your control. Morgenstern and Shask would both be good examples.

  Step 8)
Powers

Choose your characters powers. Your character starts with 15 points in Advantages to purchase Age and Powers with. Most powers cost 1 Advantage per level, Major Powers (The Pattern and the Logrus) cost 2 Advantages per Level. Characters can also purchase Powers by spending 5 Freebie points per extra Advantage.

  Step 10)
Merits and Flaws

APTITUDES AWARENESS MENTAL PHYSICAL PSYCHOLOGICAL SOCIAL SUPERNATURAL

Characters can spend Freebie points on Merits for their characters, or gain a few extra points by taking flaws. Players should probably shouldn't spend more then 10 points in merits or flaws and should avoid going overboard.


APTITUDES

Ability Aptitude (2 pt. Merit) You have exceptional talent with one specific non-combat ability. You are two Levels up in any challenge relating to that ability.

Computer Aptitude (1 pt. Merit) You have a natural affinity with computers, putting you two Levels up in any challenge involving computer use.

Daredevil (3 pt. Merit) You have exceptional luck when trying daring or stupid things. You are one Level up on all challenges in which you try something dangerous.

Illiterate (1 pt. Flaw) You cannot read or write in any language.

Jack of All Trades (5 pt. Merit) You have picked up a little bit of everything, and may attempt any ability-related action as if you had a single level of that ability.

Pitiable (1 pt. Merit) There is something about you that others pity, causing many to care for you as if you were a child.

AWARENESS

Acute Sense (1 pt. Merit) Your sense of sight, hearing, touch, taste, or smell (choose one) is exceptionally acute, making you two Levels up in all challenges directly relating to the acuity of your sense.

Bad Sight (2 pt. Flaw) You have an uncorrectable vision defect that makes you two Levels down on all vision tests.

Blind (6 pt. Flaw) You cannot see anything at all. Ever.

Deaf (3 pt. Flaw) You cannot hear any sound regardless of its volume.

Hard of Hearing (1 pt. Flaw) Your hearing is poor, and you are two Levels down on any hearing challenge.

One Eye (2 pt. Flaw) You have only one working eye. On your blind side, you have no peripheral vision. Your depth perception is also terrible, making you two Levels down on any task requiring depth perception.

MENTAL

Absent-minded (3 pt. Flaw) You forget names, addresses, where you left your keys, etc.

Amnesia (2 pt. Flaw) Your past is a blank slate. The only things that you know about yourself are those that are obvious, or that you can see in a mirror.

Confused (2 pt. Flaw) You are constantly confused. The world is a bewildering place, and you are unable to make sense of things.

Eidetic Memory (2 pt. Merit) You have a near-perfect memory. By concentrating on the memory for a round, you can commit a page of text, an image or a brief conversation to memory.

Iron Will (5 pt. Merit) You are immune to all but the most powerful Mental Domination Spells or Powers.

Weak-willed (3 pt. Flaw) You are two Levels down when resisting all Dominate and intimidation challenges. Further, you may only spend Willpower when your survival is at stake.


PHYSICAL

Addiction (3 pt. Flaw)

Ambidextrous (1 pt. Merit) You may use both hands equally well, eliminating the penalty for off hand tool use. This does not give you extra actions.

Catlike Balance (1 pt. Merit) You almost never trip or stumble, and are two Levels up in situations where balance is the deciding factor. In addition, you may walk on narrow ledges, taught wires, and slippery surfaces without having to test to see if you fall.

Deformity (3 pt. Flaw) You have some physical deformity that makes you physically and socially disadvantaged. You are two Levels down on all physical and social tests.

Disfigured (2 pt. Flaw) You are hideously ugly, suffering a two Level penalty on all social tests that are not intimidation based.

Huge Size (4 pt. Merit) You are exceptionally large (300+ lbs), and therefore get an extra Bruised health level.

Lame (3 pt. Flaw) You have severe leg damage and can only take one step per action, and can never move faster than a slow walk.

Monstrous (3 pt. Flaw) You somehow have a visage worthy of a Demon.

Mute (4 pt. Flaw) You cannot speak or make any intelligible sounds.


PSYCHOLOGICAL

Berserker (2 pt. Merit) You may enter a Berserker rage at will. You may not flee combat, but ignore all injury penalties.

Code of Honor (1 pt. Merit) You have a strict moral code to which you adhere. When something would make you go against your code, you are two Levels up to resist. However, breaking your code causes you anguish, and may result in loss of Willpower or other negative effects. Work with the GM to develop your specific Code of Honor.

Compulsion (1 pt. Flaw) You have psychological compulsion for some activity, be it gambling, lying, cleanliness, stealing, etc.

Driving Goal (3 pt. Flaw) You have some personal goal that is the center of your life. Everything you do relates to this goal, and you must always work toward it.

Hatred (3 pt. Flaw) You have a total and unreasoning hatred of a certain thing. You must attempt to destroy or control any example of the hated thing that crosses your path.

Higher Purpose (1 pt. Merit) You have a goal that drives you in everything, and gives you the strength to carry on in dire circumstances. When attempting an action that directly works toward your higher purpose, you are two Levels up on any challenges.

Intolerance (1 pt. Flaw) You have an unreasoning dislike for a certain thing.

Low Self-Image (2 pt. Flaw) You lack confidence in yourself, and are two Levels down in any situation where you don't expect to succeed. At the storyteller's option, you may have to expend Willpower to simply attempt a task that you would have little chance of succeeding at.

Nightmares (1 pt. Flaw)

Phobia: Mild (1 pt. Flaw) You have an overpowering fear of one thing or circumstance. This fear is utterly irrational, and you must expend a Willpower Point in order to remain in the vicinity of the object you fear.

Short Fuse (2 pt. Flaw) You have a bad temper and must lash out anytime you are insulted or angered.

Shy (1 pt Flaw) You are ill at ease dealing with people, and are one Level down on all challenges dealing with social dealings. You are two Levels down when the center of attention of 10+ people.

Soft-Hearted (1 pt. Flaw) You cannot stand to see others suffer. You must avoid suffering, and if you cannot avoid seeing it, you are one Level down in all challenges for one hour.

Speech Impediment (1 pt. Flaw) You have a stutter or other speech impediment.

True Love (1 pt. Merit) You have discovered a true love. This love provides joy in a torrid existence usually devoid of such enlightened emotions. Whenever you are suffering, in danger or dejected, the thought of your true love is enough to make you persevere. In game terms, it puts you two Levels up on a challenge, but only when you are actively striving to protect or come closer to your true love.

Vengeance (2 pt. Flaw) You have a score to settle, and focus on it intently. You have to spend Willpower to put your vengeance on the back burner, and even then you always come back to it. They wronged you, and they must pay.


SOCIAL

Boon (1-5 pt. Merit) Someone or something owes you a boon. Higher point cost indicates that either the boon is more serious or that the person who owes you is more powerful.

Dark Secret (1 pt Flaw) You have a secret that would have terrible consequences if it was found out.

Enemy (1-5 pt. Flaw) Someone out there is very unhappy with you. The more points you take, the more unhappy or powerful (or both) they are.

Hunted (3 pt. Flaw) Some power or group is gunning for you.

Owe a Boon (1-3 flaw) You owe a boon to someone of power, who can call it due at any time. Higher point cost indicates a more serious boon.

 
SUPERNATURAL

Eerie Presence (2 pt. Flaw) You radiate palpable evil that everyone around you feels, and clergy and devout mortals can sense that something is horribly wrong with you.

Can't Cross Running Water (3 pt. Flaw) You believe that you can't cross running water, and indeed, something prevents you from doing so. You must be at least 50 feet above a body of water to cross it. Running water is considered to be any body of water at least two feet wide in any direction and not completely stagnant and enclosed. (sewer lines under the street don't count.)

Cast No Reflection (1 pt. Flaw) You cast no reflection in mirrors or silvered surfaces.

Cursed (1-5 pt. Flaw) A powerful curse affects you. The number of Levels you take in this flaw determines the power and tenacity of the curse. A one-Level curse might cause you to stumble whenever you cross a doorstep, a three-Level curse could cause any weapon you use to break after one strike, a five-point curse might result in all your best efforts being eventually turned against you. The exact particulars of the curse are up to the storyteller.

Dark Fate (5 pt. Flaw) Some horrible end awaits you, and you cannot avert it. Worse still, you have glimpses or some foreknowledge of your demise or eternal torement. The storyteller will determine a particular fate, which will invariably strike you, removing your character from play. Furthermore, in any particular session where the Storteller deems it appropriate, you may receive a vision of your impending misery. You must spend a Willpower Point to shake off the experience, or you are a Level down on all challenges for the rest of the session. This flaw can be difficult to roleplay, and players are advised to think carefully before choosing it.

Haunted (3 pt. Flaw) A malicious ghost has decided to torment you from beyond the grave. The effects of this flaw are completely up to the storytellers.

Magic Resistance (2 pt. Merit) The magical spells, rituals and other magic seems to have difficulty affecting you. You gain two bonus Levels when testing against any form of magic, though this bonus applies to both benevolent and harmful effects. You can never learn any Magic path if you take this Merit.

Medium (2 pt. Merit) You posses a natural affinity to sense, see and hear spirits. You can speak with them if they are in the area, and even occasionally summon them to your presence through pleading and cajoling. This merit does not give any degree of control over said spirits, and they will usually expect payment for any favors done.

Prophetic Dreams (1-3 pt Merit) You have informative, though often strange, dreams. The clarity and usefulness of your dreams is based on your level of this Merit.

Repelled by Crosses (3 pt. Flaw) The sight of a cross causes you to flee unless you spend a Willpower Level (in which case you can resist it for the rest of the scene). If you are struck by a cross on your bare skin, you take one level of aggravated damage for each each blow. (This Flaw pertains to the actual religious symbol, not two crossed popsicle sticks or whatever else.)

Repulsed By Garlic (1 pt. Flaw) Even the smallest whiff of Garlic is sufficient to drive you from the room. When you are confronted by a bit of garlic, you must expend a Willpower Level or be forced at least 10 feet away. If you spend the Willpower, you are able to abide the stench for one scene.

Spirit Mentor (3 pt. Merit) A ghostly companion and guide follows you about, exercising its powers on your behalf. At the Storytellers' discretion, it can show up and aid you with information or (occasionally) direct intervention.

Taint of Corruption (1 pt. Flaw) Plants wither and die at your touch.

Luck (4 pt. merit)
