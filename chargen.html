Introduction:

Character Creation in this system is simple. I've tried to make it so that most character concepts are possible. Everyone gets the same points, (Even the NPC's and Elders for the most part. You'll encounter many people with LESS points then standard characters, but even Benedict will be built on the same number of points as everyone else, (although Dworkin may not.) Characters shouldn't have a problem making the character they want unless they just have an unreasonable concept.

Concept

There are two types of characters that you can play in this game. Books Characters, and Non Book characters. If you want to play one of the Book Characters there are a few things you should know.

1. My versions of the Book Characters and Elders are pretty simple and straight forward. I want to keep them that way. This isn't true for all, but it is true for most. Most Amberite Elders have four Attributes and Pattern. That's it. Most don't even have Sorcery. Lords of Chaos often have a wider range of powers then Princes of Amber. I don't want concepts like 'Gerard the Secret Mage of Amber' or 'Flora Shape Shifter in Disguise.'

2. I have a very Toned Down view of Amber. Princes of Amber or Lords of Chaos are not Gods. They ARE Superhuman in many ways, but they are still very mortal. A Prince of Amber can be very skilled, but they are not perfect. They still take time to Aim when they shoot a rifle, and even then they can still miss. Normal Men are also not All pushovers. A normal man can reach Amber level in something like Fencing, and even higher. And there are Human Sorcerers with the power to challenge even the Lords of Chaos. Shadow is a very dangerous place.

3. Age isn't everything. The Way I use the Attributes is different then many of the other Amber games out there. The Four Attributes in this game will represent dedication, and talent more then age and experience. Someone Young and Driven like Luke can have just as high a Warfare as someone Old and Experienced like Random or Julian. You won't see any young Amberites out fencing Benedict, but you might find one that can hold his ground.

4. Also, the Attributes have nothing to do with how skilled a character is. This is a change I've made from the normal ADRPG rules. Strength, is just that. It has nothing to do with how skilled a character is in hand to hand combat, or what martial arts and wrestling skills they know. Skills are free, and really only limited by age. Psyche and Warfare have nothing to do with how good a computer programmer your character is. This is part of your character background. Merlin knows a lot more about computers then Fiona or Benedict does.
Ask Ghostwheel.

This goes for Warfare as well. In this game, Warfare has nothing to do with what kind of a military leader you are or your skill at tactics. Benedict is the greatest military leader alive because it's part of his background. If you want to dispute that claim with him you'd better hope that you too have over a thousand years of experience, leading every type of army known, though every type of military campaign imaginable. Good luck.


The same rules apply to New Non Book characters. Keep your backgrounds and powers simple and you'll have a much better chance of being accepted.

Some concepts WILL NOT get 300 points to make their characters with. Human characters wil be built with much fewer points, as will most other shadow concepts.

Characters do not have to slave over long backgrounds or applications to be accepted. I'd like enough info to get a good feel for the character. Nothing fancy. Just give me enough info to know what you want to make and where he or she is from.

Age

Determine your characters Age. This has to be worked out with the GM and will play a large role in your characters power level and skills. Age works differently for Lords of Amber and Chaos. The rules for age are:

Age to a Prince of Amber.

Those of the Blood of Amber have almost limitless potential to learn new skills and improve on them. Their power over Pattern allows them to travel to many different worlds and learn new skills, and face new challenges. It is because of this that they have become among the most skilled and talented beings in Shadow.

Age to a Lord of Chaos.

The Shadows near the Abyss are stagnant with Chaos. In the Courts nothing is constant, the land changes before your eyes and is never the same way twice. Shadow there is easily manipulated, even human level psyche is enough to force a change in Shadow in Chaos. These changes only last while energy is devoted to them however. Because of this environment of constant change, nothing really ever changes. There is little progress, no advances in technology, and little stable resources. The only constant in Chaos is the need to survive.

For the Lords of Chaos time and dates have little meaning, age is often recognized, as is blood and power and these factors more then any other will determine a Lord of Chaos's standing in the Courts. As the Lords of Chaos age they grow in personal power gaining greater mastery in Shape Shifting and the Logrus. Sorcery is also common in Chaos and the Courts are home to many of the greatest Necromancers and Sorcerers in all of shadow.

Age for Lords of Chaos is not measured in years but instead is measured in Ages, periods of time in Chaos that were subject to great changes such as the creation of the Pattern or Rise of the Houses. It should be noted that on average the Lords of Chaos are MUCH older then the elders in Amber. The difference in age between a Lord of Chaos Elder and a Amberite Elder might well be over a hundred thousand years.

Age and Skills.

An important part of creating your character will be going over how he or she has spent their time, and listing what skills they've picked up. Obviously with some of the Elders in Amber, this can become quite a long list.

What I want to know is what skills the character could have learned. If in your character background you have listed that you've "spent more then 200 years out in shadow, letting my desires lead me and following my instincts," no one would argue that you couldn't have spent a few years as a surgeon during that time.

What I also need however is a small but detailed list of what your character is GOOD at. What types of skills has your character focused on? Everyone knows that Caine was a nasty knife fighter, Random is a mean poker player, and that Gerard is an excellent sailor. What do characters know about you?

I should point out, that in many ways the Lords of Amber are much more skilled then the Lords of Chaos. This has to do with the abilities granted to them by the Patterns and Logrus. A Prince of Amber can find any desire out in shadow. Teachers for any subject are easy for masters of the Pattern to find. Chaos Lords however have little control over what they find in shadow.
And Chaos itself has little to teach it's Lords other then Survival.

 

Attributes

Psyche:

This attribute determines a characters Mental Strength and Sensitivity. Psyche has nothing to do with the intelligence of the character. It is more a gauge of their ability in manipulating Pattern, Logrus, Magic or Trump. In addition, a character with a high Psyche will be sensitive to danger and to other things.

Psyche helps to determine the Willpower of a character, but it is not the only factor. Endurance plays a large role in any contest of Wills.

Strength:

Strength is just that. Physical Power. It's used for more then just lifting things. Strength not only determines the damage that a character inflicts in combat, but it also determines how much damage the character can take. A punch from Gerard could easily harm Corwin, despite his Endurance. A punch from Corwin would barely slow Gerard down.

"I beat that dark blade aside and my lunge was perfect. The center of its breast was my target. I ran it through."
-Corwin, The Guns of Avalon

Strength is also very useful when fighting with weapons. With a higher Strength, a character can beat an opponents weapon aside, giving him an opening. Even an inferior swordsmen can attempt to bind weapons with an enemy. They can also attempt to knock the weapon out of their opponents hand, or even break the weapon.

Beware characters with High Strength. They only need to hit you once.

Endurance:

Endurance, the most Important Attribute. Really. Here's why: Any character can force any conflict into a test of Endurance at nearly Any time. Here are some examples:

Warfare:
"Perhaps you were a fool, to enter here," I said. "You're slowing. you know," and I tried a hard, fast, vicious attack.
He parried it, but he fell back two paces in doing so.
"That wound's getting to you," I added. "Your arm's weakening. You can feel the strength leaving it-"
"Shut up!" he said, and I realized I'd gotten through to him. This increased my chances by several percent, I decided, and I pressed him as hard as I could, realizing I couldn't keep that pace up very long.
But Eric didn't realize it.
-Nine Princes in Amber

Here Corwin pushed himself to go faster, longer then Eric could. He was able to push himself to attack faster then he normally would, and he was able to maintain that pace longer then Eric because he was in better shape. Eric had no choice but to take a defensive stance to protect himself from Corwins increased attacks.

Strength:
It pinned my arms to my sides, and its horns struck against my visor, once, twice...
Then I threw my strength against those arms, and their grip began to weaken.
I dropped Grayswandir, and with a final heave broke the hold it had upon me.
-The Guns of Avalon

Here Corwin again pushed himself, exerting his full strength as long as he could in order to weaken, and then break his opponents grip. Endurance will effect contests of Strength the least, but with a higher Endurance a characters can wear down an opponent who is normally stronger then they are.

Psyche:
I thought that I would pass out from the pain, but I determined, out of sheer stubbornness and anger, to persist until I did. I concentrated on the Pattern as a dying man might cry out to his God, and I threw my entire will against the existence of the black road.
-Guns of Avalon

In this instance Corwin is striving against something with a much higher Psychic strength then his own (Or Fiona's for that matter.) Through sheer Endurance Corwin managed to overcome that portion of the Black Road and cut a path though it. It is unlikely that any of the Princes or Princesses beside Corwin or maybe Bleys would have had the Endurance to do what Corwin did.

In game terms a character can usually add the difference between his Endurance and his opponents to whatever Attribute is being used. This isn't true for all conflicts, but can be done in many. When Corwin fought Benedict for example, he was forced to push himself to the maximum in order to simply defend Benedict's attacks. From a game point of view Corwin took the difference between his Endurance and Benedicts, (about 50 points) and added it to his Warfare, (which made them about equal) Corwin however could not have kept up that pace for long and had the fight continued he would soon have slowed to the point where Benedict would have killed him.

Warfare:

The measure of a characters Speed, Dexterity, and Reflexes. Warfare in this game controls a characters Physical and Mental reaction time. It controls how fast and accurate a character is when wielding a weapon, and therefore will play a large role in determining the outcome of any combat.

Warfare in this game has nothing to do with a characters Mental Skills or Military Tactics! These skills are determined solely by the characters background, age, and picked skills.

 


Powers

Choose your characters powers. Powers in this system range in cost anywhere from 5 to 100 points. Most characters do not have more then 50 points in any one power, or 25 points in any magic skill. Many of the Advanced powers have been toned down form the Amber Decals Role Playing Game, and all have undergone some major changes.

I don't really know what to say about the versions of the Powers that will be used for this game. I've heard some described them as more powerful then those in the ADRPG, and some have said they are weaker. No Pattern Lenses here. No pattern-mind-walking-teleports. Sorcerers can be very dangerous however. And Shapeshifter are no pushovers.

I'd once again like to point out that those with Advanced abilities are very rare. Of all the Elders in Amber, less then half a dozen have more then 50 points in Pattern. Only Fiona has more then 60.

Resources

Resources are gained and lost through Roleplay and Contributions. Resources are used to invest in and rate Shadows, Items of Power, and Enchanted Creatures. They are the only points that will be gained and lost throughout the game. A 300 point character will always be a 300 point character, but his or her resources may change dramatically.

Many characters will start with certain Resources because of their background. Julian obviously will start out with his Hounds, Ardens Rangers, Morgenstern, his Armor, etc. Random has the Jewel, and Jasra the Fount of Power. This all might change once the game gets going.

Your characters application and background will effect what Resources the character begins with. Contributions to the game will greatly increase your amount of resources.

Shadows:

Shadows can become a very important part of the game. Each is unique and can provide a character with a tremendous amount of Resources. Shadows themselves have 3 attributes:

Wealth: The Amount of riches of any kind that can be gotten from the Shadow. Be it gold, jewels, information, trading, or rare goods.

Power: This is a measure of the Strength of the Shadow and it's magical power. This is a very important aspect of shadows near Chaos where shadows are so weak. The Shadow of the Fount of Power would rate high here, as would any shadow possessing a Broken Pattern or Imperfect Logrus.

Military: The Armed Forces and Military Might of a Shadow. Avalon was known for it's powerful army. And the Shadows Corwin and Bleys latter recruited armies from were both very militant.

Obviously these rankings only matter to nearby kingdoms, etc. The Wealth of a kingdom in the Black Zone would probably be worthless if taken to the Golden Circle, and vice versa. Also, while the Fount of Power is pretty impressive to the kingdoms nearby, it's power simply doesn't extend as far as the Golden Circle.
